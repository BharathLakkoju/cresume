# Behavioural / STAR Interview Guide

---

## The STAR Framework

**S**ituation — Set the scene briefly (1–2 sentences max)
**T**ask — What was your responsibility?
**A**ction — What did YOU specifically do? (most important part)
**R**esult — Quantify the outcome wherever possible

Common mistake: Spending too long on S and T, not enough on A and R.

---

## Company-Specific Behavioural Styles

### Amazon — Leadership Principles (LPs)

Amazon tests every answer against their 16 Leadership Principles. Know them.
Most-tested LPs in India SDE interviews:

- **Customer Obsession** — "Tell me about a time you went beyond requirements for the user"
- **Ownership** — "Tell me about a time you took on something outside your scope"
- **Invent and Simplify** — "Tell me about a time you simplified a complex process"
- **Deliver Results** — "Tell me about a time you delivered under pressure"
- **Dive Deep** — "Tell me about a bug you debugged that took a long time to find"
- **Earn Trust** — "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager"

Prepare 2 stories per LP. One story can often be stretched to cover multiple LPs.

### Google — Googleyness + Role-related

- Focus on collaboration, ambiguity, impact at scale
- "Tell me about a time you worked on an ambiguous problem"
- "Tell me about a disagreement with a teammate and how you resolved it"
- "What's the most impactful thing you've built?"

### Microsoft — Growth Mindset + Collaboration

- Focus on learning from failure, helping others, cross-team impact
- "Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned"
- "Tell me about a time you had to influence without authority"

### Tier-2 (Flipkart, Swiggy, Razorpay)

- Less structured than Amazon but expect 2–3 behavioural questions
- Focus on ownership, speed of execution, handling scale/pressure
- "Tell me about the most complex feature you shipped"
- "Tell me about a time you had to make a technical decision with incomplete information"

## Universal Behavioural Questions to Prepare

Prepare a strong STAR story for each:

1. Most impactful project you've built
2. Biggest technical challenge you faced and how you solved it
3. A time you disagreed with your team / manager
4. A time you failed and what you learned
5. A time you went above and beyond
6. A time you had to meet a tight deadline
7. A time you improved a process or system
8. A time you mentored someone or helped a teammate
9. Why this company? (company-specific research required)
10. Where do you see yourself in 3–5 years?

---

## Tips

- Keep answers to 2–3 minutes max
- Use "I" not "we" — interviewers want YOUR contribution
- Quantify results: "reduced latency by 40%", "cut build time from 20 min to 4 min"
- Practice out loud — don't just write stories, say them
- For Amazon specifically: write your LP stories in a doc before interviews
